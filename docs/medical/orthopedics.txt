ORTHOPEDICS — Department Reference Guide

OVERVIEW
Orthopedics deals with the musculoskeletal system including bones, joints, ligaments, tendons, and muscles. Patients with fractures, joint pain, sports injuries, and back problems are referred to orthopedics.

COMMON CONDITIONS

1. FRACTURES
Symptoms: Pain at injury site, swelling, deformity, bruising, inability to bear weight or use limb, crepitus (bone grating sound).
Types:
  - Closed (simple): skin intact
  - Open (compound): bone protrudes through skin — HIGH risk of infection
  - Stress fracture: hairline fracture from repetitive stress
  - Pathological: fracture through diseased bone (osteoporosis, cancer)
Urgency:
  - Open fracture → Emergency (risk of osteomyelitis, vascular injury)
  - Femoral neck fracture in elderly → Emergency surgery within 36 hours
  - Closed fracture, stable → Urgent orthopedics within 24-48 hours
  - Suspected spine fracture → Emergency (risk of cord injury)
Treatment: Immobilization, reduction (closed or open), internal/external fixation, casting, pain control.
Triage: Open/unstable fractures → Emergency. Closed stable fractures → Orthopedics urgent clinic.

2. OSTEOARTHRITIS (OA)
Symptoms: Gradual joint pain worsening with activity, joint stiffness (especially morning stiffness < 30 minutes), crepitus, reduced range of motion. Commonly affects knees, hips, hands, spine.
Urgency: Non-urgent. Chronic progressive condition.
Investigations: X-ray (joint space narrowing, osteophytes, subchondral sclerosis).
Treatment: Weight loss, physiotherapy, analgesics (paracetamol, NSAIDs), intra-articular corticosteroid injections, joint replacement surgery (arthroplasty) in severe cases.
Triage: Orthopedics outpatient.

3. RHEUMATOID ARTHRITIS (RA)
Symptoms: Symmetrical joint pain and swelling, prolonged morning stiffness (> 1 hour), fatigue, low-grade fever. Small joints of hands and feet typically affected first.
Urgency: Non-urgent. Requires rheumatology involvement. Orthopedics for joint damage/deformity.
Investigations: RF, anti-CCP antibodies, CRP/ESR, X-ray.
Treatment: DMARDs (methotrexate first-line), biologics (TNF inhibitors), NSAIDs for symptoms.
Triage: Rheumatology (primary) + Orthopedics for surgical management.

4. BACK PAIN — ACUTE LOW BACK PAIN
Symptoms: Pain in lumbar region, muscle spasm, limited movement.
Red flags (require urgent investigation):
  - Bilateral leg weakness or numbness
  - Bladder or bowel dysfunction (cauda equina syndrome — EMERGENCY)
  - Night pain not relieved by rest (cancer/infection)
  - History of malignancy
  - Fever + back pain (possible spinal infection)
Urgency: With red flags → Emergency. Without red flags → Conservative management.
Treatment: Paracetamol, NSAIDs, short course muscle relaxants, physiotherapy, avoid bed rest.
Triage: Cauda equina signs → Emergency. Mechanical back pain → GP / Orthopedics if persistent > 6 weeks.

5. INTERVERTEBRAL DISC PROLAPSE (SLIPPED DISC)
Symptoms: Back pain radiating to leg (sciatica), tingling or numbness in leg/foot, worse with sitting/bending/coughing. Positive straight leg raise test.
Urgency: Usually non-urgent unless neurological compromise.
Investigations: MRI spine (preferred over CT).
Treatment: Conservative (analgesics, physiotherapy) for 6-12 weeks. Surgery (microdiscectomy) if conservative treatment fails or progressive neurology.
Triage: Orthopedics or neurosurgery outpatient. Emergency if cauda equina involvement.

6. ANTERIOR CRUCIATE LIGAMENT (ACL) TEAR
Symptoms: Knee injury (usually twisting mechanism), "pop" felt/heard, rapid swelling (hemarthrosis), knee instability, inability to continue activity.
Urgency: Non-urgent in isolation. Urgent if vascular injury suspected.
Investigations: MRI knee.
Treatment: RICE (rest, ice, compression, elevation), physiotherapy for rehabilitation, surgical reconstruction if significant instability.
Triage: Orthopedics outpatient. Emergency if vascular compromise.

7. SHOULDER DISLOCATION
Symptoms: Deformity of shoulder, severe pain, arm held in abduction/external rotation, loss of deltoid contour, inability to move shoulder.
Urgency: Urgent — requires reduction, ideally within 6 hours.
Treatment: Sedation/analgesia, closed reduction (Cunningham, Kocher techniques), sling for 3-6 weeks, physiotherapy.
Triage: Emergency department for reduction. Orthopedics follow-up for rehabilitation.

8. CARPAL TUNNEL SYNDROME
Symptoms: Tingling, numbness, pain in hand and fingers (especially thumb, index, middle finger). Worse at night, relieved by shaking hand. Weakness of grip in advanced cases.
Urgency: Non-urgent.
Investigations: Nerve conduction studies.
Treatment: Wrist splints (especially at night), steroid injections, surgical carpal tunnel release if conservative fails.
Triage: Orthopedics outpatient.

9. TENDINOPATHY (TENDINITIS)
Examples: Rotator cuff tendinopathy, Achilles tendinopathy, patellar tendinopathy, lateral epicondylitis (tennis elbow).
Symptoms: Pain over tendon, worsened by activity, tender on palpation, no swelling in early stages.
Urgency: Non-urgent.
Treatment: Activity modification, eccentric exercises, physiotherapy, NSAIDs, steroid injections.
Triage: Orthopedics or sports medicine outpatient.

EMERGENCY ORTHOPEDIC CONDITIONS
- Open (compound) fracture — skin broken over fracture
- Vascular injury with fracture (cold, pulseless limb)
- Compartment syndrome: severe pain, tight swollen limb, pain with passive stretch → Emergency fasciotomy
- Cauda equina syndrome: bilateral leg weakness + urinary/bowel dysfunction
- Septic arthritis: hot, swollen joint + fever → Emergency joint washout
- Spinal cord injury: any neurological deficit after trauma

MEDICATIONS IN ORTHOPEDICS
- Paracetamol: first-line analgesic
- NSAIDs (ibuprofen, diclofenac, naproxen): anti-inflammatory, analgesia
- Tramadol, codeine: moderate pain
- Morphine: severe acute pain
- Corticosteroid injections: local anti-inflammatory
- Calcium + Vitamin D: osteoporosis prevention/treatment
- Bisphosphonates (alendronate): osteoporosis treatment
- Tranexamic acid: reduces surgical blood loss

DIAGNOSTIC TOOLS
- X-ray: fractures, joint degeneration, alignment
- MRI: soft tissue (ligaments, tendons, cartilage, discs, spinal cord)
- CT: complex fractures, 3D surgical planning
- Ultrasound: tendons, soft tissue, guided injections
- Bone scan: stress fractures, metastases
- Arthroscopy: diagnostic and therapeutic joint procedure

TRIAGE SUMMARY
- Bone fracture with skin intact → Orthopedics urgent
- Open fracture / vascular compromise → Emergency
- Knee injury + joint instability → Orthopedics outpatient
- Back pain + leg weakness + bladder dysfunction → Emergency (cauda equina)
- Joint swelling + fever → Emergency (septic arthritis)
- Chronic joint pain → Orthopedics outpatient
- Shoulder dislocation → Emergency for reduction
